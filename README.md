# Subtype Discovery in PCOS Patients  
### An Unsupervised Learning and Explainability Approach

This repository contains the research work titled **"Subtype Discovery in PCOS Patients: An Unsupervised Learning and Explainability Approach."**

The study focuses on identifying meaningful **subtypes of Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)** patients using **unsupervised machine learning techniques** and **explainable AI methods** applied to clinical biomarker data.

---

## Research Overview

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a complex endocrine disorder that affects women with varying hormonal, metabolic, and reproductive characteristics.

Traditional diagnostic methods often fail to capture the heterogeneity among patients. This research explores **data-driven approaches** to identify hidden patient groups and better understand the underlying variations in PCOS.

---

## Methodology

The study follows a structured data science workflow:

1. **Data Preprocessing**
   - Handling missing values
   - Data normalization
   - Outlier treatment

2. **Feature Engineering**
   - Selection of relevant clinical biomarkers
   - Standardization of variables

3. **Dimensionality Reduction**
   - Principal Component Analysis (PCA)

4. **Clustering Algorithms**
   - K-Means Clustering
   - Gaussian Mixture Models
   - Agglomerative Clustering

5. **Evaluation Metrics**
   - Silhouette Score
   - Cluster separation analysis

6. **Explainability**
   - SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)

---

## Key Findings

The analysis revealed **two dominant PCOS patient subtypes**:

- **Lean PCOS**
- **Metabolic / Insulin-Resistant PCOS**

These findings highlight the importance of **personalized diagnosis and treatment strategies** based on patient-specific metabolic profiles.
